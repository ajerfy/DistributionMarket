Differences Between This Iteration and the Theoretical Distribution Market

- This implementation supports a small parameterized family of distributions, mainly Normal first and Uniform second, rather than arbitrary admissible payoff functions over a continuous outcome space.
- Market state is represented by a scaled distribution object `f(x) = lambda * p(x)` instead of a truly general function-space position that can encode multimodal, skewed, or highly irregular beliefs.
- Collateral is computed numerically with search heuristics tuned to the Normal case, so it is practical for simulation but not a proof-grade global optimizer for all valid distributions.
- The AMM reserve function `h(x) = b - f(x)` is implicit rather than modeled as a first-class object with independent accounting and queries.
- LP accounting is simplified to proportional shares and cash balances instead of a full ledger of function-native claims and withdrawals across all exposures.
- Trader positions are stored as transitions between old and new market states, which is enough for payout calculation but not yet a full transferable position system with netting and partial closes.
- Resolution is a direct payout computation at a realized outcome, without oracle/dispute machinery, settlement latency, or production-grade bookkeeping constraints.
- The math uses `f64`, which is suitable for research and iteration but not for invariant-critical or onchain-safe execution.
- Uniform support exists, but the numerical search and validation path are still designed primarily around the Normal-family interpretation from the paper.

What the Final Any-Valid-Distribution Implementation Should Look Like and How to Reach It

- The final market core should represent positions as validated payoff or density functions, not just named parametric distributions, so any admissible distribution can define `p(x)` and therefore `f(x) = lambda * p(x)`.
- The API should separate exact analytic families from numerically validated families, allowing closed-form methods when available and robust numerical fallbacks when they are not.
- A canonical admissible representation is needed for arbitrary distributions, most likely mixtures of basis distributions, splines, or piecewise densities rather than opaque user-defined black boxes.
- Every candidate distribution should pass a validation pipeline covering non-negativity, normalization, finite L2 norm, max-density bounds, and solvency after scaling.
- Collateral search needs to be upgraded from family-specific heuristics to a more robust global minimization framework with adaptive bracketing, multi-start search, and conservative verification.
- LP accounting should become function-native, so LP deposits and withdrawals correspond to explicit proportional ownership of both the market exposure and collateral pool.
- Trader positions should be promoted into first-class objects storing their payoff delta `g(x) - f(x)`, posted collateral, and settlement state to support richer composition later.
- The codebase should add approximation backends that converge toward the same theory, such as exact symbolic families, high-precision numerical families, and discretized grid approximations.
- The next practical milestone is to add Gaussian-mixture support, because mixtures of Normals are expressive enough to approximate many arbitrary distributions while staying close to the current design.
- Once the representation and verification model are stable, the final step is to define a bounded serialized format that preserves these guarantees in a future Solana port.
